Place of Articulation

Bilabial

Produced using both lips (e.g., sounds like /p/, /b/).

Labiodental

Produced by the lower lip and upper teeth. Examples: /f/, /v/.

Interdental

Produced by placing the tongue between the upper and lower teeth. Examples: /θ/ as in "think", /ð/ as in "this".

Alveolar ridge

The raised area just behind the upper front teeth, used to produce many sounds. Examples: /t/, /d/, /s/, /z/, /n/, /l/.

Hard Plate

The hard part of the roof of the mouth, used in palatal sounds. Example: /j/ as in "yes".

Velar

Produced by the back of the tongue against the soft palate (velum). Examples: /k/, /g/, /ŋ/ as in "sing".

Glottal

Produced using the vocal folds or the space between them (the glottis). Examples: /h/ as in "hat".
