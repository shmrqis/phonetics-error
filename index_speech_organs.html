Speech Organs

Place of Articulation Manner of Articulation Phonation

Mouth

The mouth is the primary resonating chamber in speech production. It shapes sound waves and contains many critical articulators.

Teeth

Teeth are important for producing dental and labiodental sounds. They work with the tongue and lips to create sounds like /θ/ and /f/.

Tongue

The tongue is one of the most versatile speech organs. It can move in many directions to form different places of articulation.

Palate

The hard palate and soft palate (velum) separate the oral and nasal cavities and play a role in sounds like /j/ and /k/.

Epiglottis

The epiglottis covers the windpipe during swallowing. In some languages, it can contribute to epiglottal consonants.

Larynx

The larynx houses the vocal folds and is essential for phonation. It controls pitch and volume.

Vocal Cords

Also called vocal folds, these tissues vibrate to create voiced sounds. Their position determines voicing in speech.
